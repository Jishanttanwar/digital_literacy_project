## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of online financial fraud where criminals trick victims into authorising payments or revealing sensitive information through manipulated QR codes, fake payment links, or social engineering.

---

## Case Study: The "Receive Money" Scam

**Scenario (Realistic Fictional Case)**

Rohit, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, posted on an online marketplace in December 2024 that he was selling his old laptop for ₹18,000. A buyer contacted him on WhatsApp and expressed interest.

**Step-by-Step: How the Fraud Happened**

1. The "buyer" asked for Rohit's UPI ID to send an advance of ₹5,000.
2. A few minutes later, the buyer sent a **QR code via WhatsApp**, claiming it was a "payment confirmation code" and asked Rohit to scan it to receive the money.
3. Rohit scanned the QR code in his Google Pay app. A prompt appeared asking for his UPI PIN — which he entered, believing it was to accept the payment.
4. Instead, ₹5,000 was **debited** from Rohit's account, not credited. Scanning a QR code in UPI always initiates a *payment*, not a receipt.
5. The fraudster disappeared. Rohit lost ₹5,000 and never sold the laptop.

**Who is Usually Targeted?**

Students and young adults selling items on OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or Instagram; people unfamiliar with how UPI QR codes actually work.

**Consequences**

Financial loss, emotional distress, delayed recovery since transactions are instant and hard to reverse. Rohit reported the incident on **cybercrime.gov.in** and called **1930**, but the money was not recovered.
