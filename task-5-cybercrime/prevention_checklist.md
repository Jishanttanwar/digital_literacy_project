# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 5 | Jishant Tanwar (25BHI10093)**

---

A practical checklist for college students in India to stay protected from online fraud and cybercrime.

---

##  General Digital Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
   Combine uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager (e.g., Bitwarden) instead of reusing the same password everywhere.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
   Gmail, Instagram, GitHub, and banking apps all support 2FA. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks the attacker.

3. **Never click on unverified links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email.**
   Fraudsters disguise links as OTP pages, prize claim portals, or bank login pages. Always type URLs directly into your browser.

4. **Verify the identity of anyone asking for money or personal information.**
   Call them back on a known number. Do not rely solely on WhatsApp or texts for verification.

5. **Keep your devices and apps updated.**
   Software updates patch security vulnerabilities. Delaying updates leaves your device exposed to known attacks.

6. **Do not use public Wi-Fi for financial transactions.**
   Public Wi-Fi at cafes, malls, or railway stations can be intercepted. Use mobile data or a VPN for banking.

---

##  UPI & Financial Safety (Specific to Indian Students)

7. **Scanning a QR code always SENDS money — it never receives it.**
   This is the most common UPI myth exploited by fraudsters. If someone sends you a QR code "to receive payment," it is a scam. To receive money, you only need to share your UPI ID.

8. **Your UPI PIN is like your ATM PIN — never share it with anyone, under any circumstances.**
   No bank employee, payment app representative, or government official will ever ask for your UPI PIN. If anyone does, disconnect immediately.

---

##  Where to Report Cybercrime in India

| Resource | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) – File a complaint online 24×7 |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** – Call 24×7 for urgent financial fraud cases |
| **Local Police** | Visit your nearest police station or file an e-FIR |

> **Important:** Report financial fraud on the 1930 helpline as quickly as possible. Quick reporting increases the chance of freezing fraudulent transactions before the money is withdrawn.
