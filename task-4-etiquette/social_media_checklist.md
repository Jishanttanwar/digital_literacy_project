# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 4 | Aryan Sharma (24BCE0001)**

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.
2. **Keep your LinkedIn profile updated** – Add new courses, certifications, and projects at least once a semester.
3. **Use your real name professionally** – On LinkedIn, GitHub, and Kaggle, use your full legal name so recruiters can find you.
4. **Credit others' work** – If you share someone's code, article, or photo, always tag or credit the original creator.
5. **Use strong privacy settings** – Review who can see your posts on Instagram and Facebook. Set personal accounts to Private.
6. **Engage positively in communities** – Comment constructively on LinkedIn posts, GitHub issues, and Stack Overflow questions. Your comments are part of your digital footprint.
7. **Report cyberbullying** – If you witness or experience harassment online, use the platform's report feature and contact cybercrime.gov.in if needed.
8. **Separate personal and professional accounts** – Use one account for casual posts (friends, memes) and a separate professional one for academic/career networking.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share your location in real time** – Posting "at XYZ restaurant right now" can make you a target for crime or stalking.
2. **Don't post offensive or controversial content** – Even "jokes" can be taken out of context and damage your reputation years later. Screenshots last forever.
3. **Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers** – Especially those with few followers, no profile picture, or suspicious bios.
4. **Don't share screenshots of private conversations** – This is a serious breach of trust and can lead to college disciplinary action.
5. **Don't use social media to spread unverified news** – Always check the source before sharing. Spreading misinformation can have legal consequences.
6. **Don't overshare personal information** – Your phone number, home address, Aadhaar details, or bank information should never appear on any social platform.
7. **Don't engage with trolls or cyberbullies** – Responding gives them attention. Block, report, and move on.
8. **Don't post during exams or official college events** – It reflects poorly on your professionalism and focus.
