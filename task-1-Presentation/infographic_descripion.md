# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic

## Tool Used
**Canva** (canva.com) was used to design the infographic. The HTML version in this folder is a faithful recreation of the same design for digital submission.

## What the Design Shows
The infographic is a single-page visual resource titled **"Digital Literacy – A Guide for First-Year Students"**. It is divided into six colour-coded sections:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?** – Definition and key dimensions
2. **Safe Internet Practices** – Six actionable safety tips
3. **Professional Online Presence** – Platforms and habits for building a digital profile
4. **Useful Digital Tools** – Five recommended tools for students (Canva, Google Drive, HackerRank, Notion, Grammarly)
5. **Email Etiquette** – Six rules for professional communication
6. **Cybercrime Warning Signs** – Red flags and the national helpline (1930)

A statistics bar at the bottom provides four impactful data points to drive home why digital literacy matters.

## Reflection
Designing this infographic taught me how much thought goes into visual communication. The most interesting challenge was deciding what *not* to include — there was so much to say about digital literacy, but too much text makes an infographic unreadable. I had to prioritise the six most important topics and express each one in under six bullet points. The most difficult part was choosing a colour scheme that looked professional without being boring.
